Frequently Asked Questions

How does a hydraulic press brake work?

A hydraulic press brake uses hydraulic pressure to apply force to a punch, which bends the sheet metal against a die.

What are the common problems encountered when using a hydraulic press brake?

Common problems with hydraulic press brakes include oil leaks, hydraulic cylinder issues, and misalignment of the ram.

What is the difference between a hydraulic press brake and a mechanical press brake?

The main difference between hydraulic and mechanical press brakes lies in the method of applying force—hydraulic brakes use hydraulic pressure, while mechanical brakes use a mechanical lever.

How do you troubleshoot hydraulic press brake issues?

Troubleshooting hydraulic press brake issues involves diagnosing hydraulic system problems, inspecting tooling, and checking machine alignment.
